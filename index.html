About the Kingdom

Discover the Wonders of Jordan!

From ancient cities to stunning deserts, Jordan is full of hidden gems waiting to be explored. Let's take a journey!

"Marhaba" — Hello!

Is Jordan Safe?

Jordan is very safe for tourists. You can explore cities, nature reserves, and deserts without worry.

Tourists often praise the welcoming and helpful locals, making their stay even more memorable.

Is Jordan Expensive?

Eating local food is cheap — a falafel sandwich costs less than $1!

By choosing local restaurants and public transportation, visitors can experience Jordan without spending a fortune.

Is Jordanian Food Good?

The food is legendary! From Mansaf (the national dish) to fresh falafel and hummus, your taste buds will be delighted.

Don't forget to try the sweet Kunafa in downtown Amman — it's the ultimate Jordanian dessert experience.

Is English Spoken in Jordan?

Yes, English is widely understood, especially in tourist areas and among the younger generation.

While Arabic is the official language, most Jordanians are happy to communicate in English.

Is Jordan Full of History?

Jordan is an open-air museum, home to Nabataeans, Romans, Byzantines, and Umayyads — civilizations that shaped the world.

Visit the Roman Theatre in Amman or the ancient city of Jerash to see history come alive before your eyes.

Is Jordan Culturally Diverse?

Jordan is a model of religious coexistence, where mosques and churches stand side-by-side in harmony.

Visitors are welcomed regardless of background, reflecting a deep tradition of hospitality — "Diyafa" — that defines Jordanian culture.

Is Jordan Only a Desert?

Not at all! Jordan has lush green pine and oak forests in Ajloun, the unique Dead Sea shoreline, and the vibrant coral reefs of Aqaba.

From diving in Aqaba to canyoning in Wadi Mujib, the landscape is incredibly diverse from north to south.

Is Wadi Rum Worth It?

Absolutely! Wadi Rum is so otherworldly that NASA uses it to simulate Martian landscapes, and it's been the filming location for many blockbuster movies.

Sleeping under the stars in a Bedouin camp here is one of the most unforgettable experiences on Earth.

Is Petra Really a Wonder?

Yes — Petra is one of the New Seven Wonders of the World, and the UNESCO calls it "one of the most precious cultural properties of man's cultural heritage."

The Treasury (Al-Khazneh) is carved entirely into a rose-red cliff and stands 43 metres tall — a true feat of ancient engineering.
